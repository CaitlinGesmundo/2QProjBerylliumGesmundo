Inflammatory Bowel Disease: Symptoms, Treatments, Differences Between Types

Treatments

Of course, treatments vary from case to case. Milder cases may use antibiotics and anti-inflammatory drugs to alleviate symptoms. Steroids are also often used. When in remission, patients take maintenance medicine with the goal of avoiding recurrance.

Ulcerative colitis may be cured (or at least alleviated) when the colon is surgically removed. Crohn's disease usually returns when the affected section is removed.

The most useful and least expensive treatment, however, is a lifestyle change. Dietary adjustments and proper hydration help greatly in managing symptoms.

If controlled adequately and properly, living with IBD might not significantly alter your day-to-day life.
