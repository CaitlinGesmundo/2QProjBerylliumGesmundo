Inflammatory Bowel Disease: Symptoms, Treatments, Differences Between Types

Home

Crohn's disease affects as many 780,000 Americans but is barely even talked about in the Philippines. 23.8 per 100,000 people in the Philippines are diagnosed with some sort of IBD, and yet it takes months—even years—to diagnose. This affects the lives and careers of those affected by the disease negatively, as it interferes with work and school. Misdiagnosis is caused by lack of education and lack of knowledgeable doctors, and these factors can be detrimental to patients suffering from these disorders.

This site serves as a basic rundown of the disease and others related to it.

DISCLAIMER:
This site is not a substitute for professional advice. If in doubt, always seek the advice of qualified healthcare providers!

Crohn's disease is a type of inflammatory bowel disease (IBD). Symptoms include diarrhea, abdominal cramps, bloody stool, fever, fatigue, loss of appetite, and weight loss.

Many diseases are included under the IBD umbrella. It represents a group of intestinal disorders that cause inflammation in the digestive tract.
